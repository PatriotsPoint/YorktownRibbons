Commendations & Battle Ribbons USS Yorktown (cv-10)

Presidential Unit Citation

Awarded for: gallantry, determination, and esprit de corps in accomplishing its mission under extremely difficult and hazardous conditions.

USS Laffey was awarded the Presidential Unit Citation for her crew's actions on April 16, 1945. At 8:30am she was about 30 mi (26 nmi; 48 km) north of Okinawa when about 50 imperial Japanese planes attacked the ship. Despite being badly damaged by four bombs, six kamikaze crashes, and strafing fire that killed 32 and wounded 71, the USS Laffey stayed afloat earning her the nickname "The ship that would not die."

China Service Medal

Awarded for: Services performed during operations in China or in Chinese territorial waters or contiguous ocean areas from 7 July 1937 to 7 September 1939 and 2 September 1945 and 1 April 1957

The USS Laffey received the Meritorious Unit Commendation in 1970 for her involvement in the Jordanian Civil War, also called 'Black September.' During this conflict, which lasted between September 16 to September 27 1970, the ship acted as a screening destroyer and a radar picket ship.

American Campaign Medal

Eligibility: U.S. military service in the American Theater for at least 30 days outside the U.S. or 1 year inside the U.S. between December 7, 1941 and March 2, 1946.

USS Laffey would receive this award first for her involvement in the D-day Invasion of Normandy, France in the June of 1944. During this engagement she would act as a gunfire-support ship. In late June Laffey would earn a battle star for her involvement during the Battle of Cherbourg. Here she was assigned to Task Force 129, Group Two. Her mission was to complete the bombardment of Cherbourg, the inner harbor forts, and the area west towards the Atlantic. During this Laffey took a dud in her port bow by the anchor. The damage control party pried it loose and tossed it overboard.

Asiatic Pacific Campaign Medal with 11 Battle Stars

Eligibility: Served in the U.S. armed forces for at least 30 days in the Asiatic-Pacific Theater between December 7, 1941 and March 2, 1946.

USS Laffey would earn the Asiatic Pacific Campaign Medal with 4 battle stars for her actions at Ormoc Bay, Mindoro, Leyte Gulf, Luzon, and Okinawa. In Ormoc Bay she operated with the 7th Fleet where she screened the big ships against submarine and air attacks. She also covered the landings at Ormoc Bay on December 7th. During this she silenced a shore battery and shelled enemy troop concentrations. In Mindoro Laffey supported the troop landings on December 15. She took part in the Battle of Leyte in October of 1944. This is considered to be the largest naval battle of the Second World War. In Luzon she screened amphibious ships that were landing troops in the Lingayen Gulf. In Okinawa she would join Task Force 54 where she would take part in the pre invasion bombardment of Okinawa and would support the initial landing there.

World War II Victory Medal

Awarded for: service in the armed forces between the dates of 7 December 1941 and 31 December 1946.

During World War Two, USS Laffey served in both the Atlantic and Pacific Theaters. In the Atlantic she served as a gunfire-support ship during the invasion of Normandy in June 1944. In the Pacific she joined the screen of Task Force 38 conducting air strikes against shipping, aircraft, and airfields in the Philippines. She took part in the Battle of Leyte in October of 1944. She would join Task Force 54 where she would take part in the pre invasion bombardment of Okinawa and would support the initial landing there.

National Defense Service Medal with 2 Battle Stars

Eligibility: Member of the United States Armed Forces during qualifying periods of national emergency Awarded for: Military service during periods of national emergency or any other periods designated by the Secretary of Defense.

During the Korean conflict USS Laffey served as the flagship for Destroyer Division 261. She would later join Task Force 77. In February 1954 she would join Task Force 75 for patrol-blockade-plane-guard duty until June.
